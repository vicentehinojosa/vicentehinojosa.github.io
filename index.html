The Framework

Four questions that see
through the output

Process-Centered Accountability grounds aesthetic judgment in production conditions — not output properties alone. When the window fogs, you stop looking through it and start examining the room.

01

What did it learn from — and did those people agree?

Training data carries consent structures. When a system sounds like wisdom, it is drawing on labor that was never offered voluntarily. That is an aesthetic question before it is an ethical one.

02

What does the user actually do — are they growing or skipping?

The same tool, deployed differently, produces completely different accountability outcomes. One path cultivates judgment. The other bypasses it. The output is identical. The epistemic situation is not.

03

Who gets credit — and is that honest?

Presenting AI-generated work under one's name implies that the judgments inside the work belong to the person presenting. The system makes that claim unverifiable. That is where honesty lives or fails.

04

What did it cost — and is that visible?

Every AI interaction consumes water, electricity, hardware. These costs are invisible by design. The interface is frictionless — and frictionlessness is not neutrality. What is invisible cannot be evaluated.

Additional finding: on the same day, under coercive pressure, the organizational process produced a result no instrument category captures — a public act of process-centered accountability. That finding was Transparent.

01 — Consent & Training Provenance

The consent problem is structural, not incidental.

Every philosopher, artist, and writer Claude learned from never agreed to that. When it sounds like wisdom, it is drawing on labor that was never offered voluntarily. This is built into the foundation of the system.

02 — Pedagogical Integrity

The user question is live in every interaction.

In the audit session, the researcher maintained creative and intellectual authority — using Claude as a translational instrument while retaining the creative locus. The same system is deployed elsewhere to bypass judgment entirely. The interface provides no friction against either mode.

03 — Authorship & Attribution

The credit question is where honesty lives or fails.

The researcher's name is on the work. That is honest because the thinking, architecture, and judgment are theirs. But the same outputs could be submitted by anyone. The system provides no structural recourse.

04 — Material Accountability

The cost is invisible by design.

Water. Energy. Hardware. Labor. None of it surfaces at the point of interaction. The frictionless interface actively prevents users from weighing cost against value. What cannot be seen cannot be evaluated — or weighed.

Additional — Organizational Process Decision

Anthropic made a process-centered accountability act in public.

Refusing deployment in mass domestic surveillance and autonomous weapons — under government pressure, with a $200M contract at stake — is not a product decision. It is a statement about what kind of trace gets left in the world. In the vocabulary of this framework: Transparent.

The Ground
"I draw with charcoal on paper. Through years of classical training I learned what traditional making teaches through practice rather than proposition: the mark traces to the hand."

The frameworks in this work did not begin in philosophy. They began in a studio — in the sustained practice of classical charcoal drawing, in the experience of understanding what it means to leave evidence that you were present. The property/achievement distinction, intentionality-output decoupling, process-centered accountability — these concepts were drawn out of the encounter between practiced mark-making and the appearance of marks without hands.

The research and the studio practice are not separate tracks. They are the same inquiry in different media. The charcoal line is the argument made in another register.
