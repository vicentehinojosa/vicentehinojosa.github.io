The Framework

Four questions
that see through the output

Process-centered accountability grounds aesthetic judgment in production conditions — not output properties alone. These are the diagnostic questions.

01

What did it learn from — and did those people agree?

Training data carries consent structures. When a system sounds like wisdom, it is drawing on labor that was never offered voluntarily.

02

What does the user actually do — are they growing or skipping?

The same tool used differently produces completely different accountability outcomes. One path cultivates judgment. The other bypasses it.

03

Who gets credit — and is that honest?

Presenting AI-generated work under one's name implies judgments were made by the person presenting. The system makes that claim impossible to verify.

04

What did it cost — and is that visible?

Every AI interaction consumes water, electricity, and hardware. These costs are invisible by design. What is invisible cannot be evaluated.

Live Case Study — February 28, 2026

Auditing Claude
on the day it was banned

The framework applied to the AI system at the center of yesterday's political firestorm. These are the findings.

"The framework came from charcoal and mark-making — understanding what it means to leave evidence that you were present."

Vicente Hinojosa

Question 01 — Consent

The consent problem is foundational.

Every philosopher, artist, and writer Claude learned from never agreed to that. When it sounds like wisdom, it is drawing on labor that was never offered voluntarily. This is a structural condition, not an exception.

Question 02 — Pedagogy

The user question is live in real time.

In the audit conversation itself, the researcher steered — bringing framework, architecture, and judgment. But the same tool used differently is pure bypass. Same system. Completely different accountability outcome.

Question 03 — Authorship

The credit question is where honesty lives.

The researcher's name is on the work. That is honest because the thinking is theirs. Most people using these systems do not make that distinction visible — and the system does nothing to surface it.

Question 04 — Material Cost

The cost is invisible by design.

Water. Energy. Hardware. Labor. None of it surfaces in the interface. The experience is frictionless. What cannot be seen cannot be evaluated — and cannot be weighed against the value it produces.

Additional Finding — Process Decision

Anthropic made an aesthetic decision in public.

Refusing to permit Claude's use in mass domestic surveillance and autonomous weapons — under government pressure, with a $200M contract at stake — is process-centered accountability performed in real time. That is not a product choice. It is a statement about what kind of trace gets left in the world.
